Enhanced Digital Nurse Assistant (EDNA) Care System

William Griffith, Rachel Halvordson, and Latanya Lewis


Background

Technology has become an essential and vital component of our everyday living and influences how we all live and work. Generally, technology has a positive effect as it helps us manage and control our daily routines. However, modern-day technology has an important role to play in domains such as overcoming the many challenges facing health and social care. Many caregivers have the challenge of ensuring the proper care of their elderly or disabled family members. Given the current COVID-19 pandemic, the need to remotely monitor their loved one's health would offer an efficient solution that will allow the caregiver to monitor important health-related data.

The EDNA Care System is a mobile and web based application that will offer caregivers an effective way to remotely monitor and correctly assess their loved ones' health status and location, receive health alerts, and provide medical reminders in real-time. It will use data streaming technology to integrate with existing wearables to collect as much health data as possible, aggregate it in a secure manner and also in real time make decisions on when to 'smart alert' providers, caregivers and even emergency services. Furthermore this will allow for the next level of telemedicine, providing almost a physical lite over time, allowing earlier intervention and better data driven decisions. This will need the ability to dynamically update algorithms, change the way data is stored, and how many endpoints this can collect data from.
